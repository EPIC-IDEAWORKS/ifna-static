Regional Community Energy:
An Online Platform

Developed with and for First Nations to reduce costs, emissions, and help the transition to renewable energy.

How do First Nation Communities benefit?

Up-to-date
Information

Have always available updated information of your community to make energy decisions

Applying for
Project Funding

Project proposals require baseline energy information at the building or community level

Look in One
Single Place

Intgrate information from different sources in one location*


Renewable Energy
Projects

Generation data from renewable energy projects can be integrated into the platform*

Capacity
Building

The platform can help inform Chief & Council, as well as community members

Compare
Buildings

Compare buildings with similar use in your community, your region or across the country


*The entry data process highly depends on available format and process from the community. The process can vary from very time consuming to automatic as more monitoring technology is available at the community

Communities We Are Working With

The Independent First Nation Alliance (IFNA); based in Sioux Lookout & Thunder Bay, Ontario; has been leading the effort to involve their member communities into the platform. To date, IFNA has enrolled 4 First Nation communities in the pilot phase of the project.

Participating Communities: Whitesand First Nation & Kitchenuhmaykoosib Inninuwug First Nation

Engaged Communities: Muskrat Dam First Nation & Lac Seul First Nation.
